The algebra
Let M = month number, D = day number Step 1: M × 5 = 5M Step 2: + 6 = 5M + 6 Step 3: × 4 = 20M + 24 Step 4: + 9 = 20M + 33 Step 5: × 5 = 100M + 165 Step 6: + D = 100M + 165 + D ───────────────────────────── Subtract 165 → 100M + D Last 2 digits = D ← your day Remaining digits = M ← your month
Mind-reader mode
Ask a friend to do the steps on their birthday and tell you the final number. Enter it here to decode their date instantly.

The trick works because the operations create 100M + D inside the result (plus a constant 165 you subtract). In base 10, multiplying by 100 pushes M two decimal places left — leaving D in the units. The structure of our number system makes this possible.
